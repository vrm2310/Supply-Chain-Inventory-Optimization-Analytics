# 1. Project Overview

## Objective

The Supply Chain & Inventory Optimization Analytics Platform is an end-to-end analytics engineering project designed to analyze supply chain operations, inventory performance, customer purchasing behavior, logistics efficiency, and overall business profitability.

The project simulates the workflow commonly followed by analytics consulting firms and enterprise business intelligence teams by integrating Python, PostgreSQL, SQL, and Power BI into a single analytics pipeline.

The primary objective is to transform raw transactional supply chain data into executive-ready insights that support strategic decision-making.

---

# 11. Business Definitions

## ABC Classification

Products are classified using Pareto Analysis based on cumulative revenue contribution.

| Class | Definition |
|--------|------------|
| A | First 80% of cumulative revenue |
| B | Next 15% of cumulative revenue |
| C | Remaining 5% of cumulative revenue |

ABC classification is commonly used for inventory optimization and inventory prioritization.

---

## Product Velocity

Measures demand intensity based on total quantity sold.

| Category | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| Fast | High demand products |
| Medium | Moderate demand products |
| Slow | Low demand products |

---

## Inventory Priority

Business priority assigned using ABC Classification and Product Velocity.

| Priority | Business Meaning |
|----------|------------------|
| Critical | Highest inventory priority |
| High | Important inventory |
| Medium | Moderate inventory importance |
| Low | Low inventory priority |

---

## Profit Margin

Percentage of revenue retained as profit after sales.

Formula

```
Profit Margin % =
Profit / Sales × 100
```

---

## Average Order Value (AOV)

Average revenue generated per order.

Formula

```
Average Order Value =
Total Revenue / Total Orders
```

---

## SLA Compliance

Indicates whether an order was delivered within the scheduled delivery timeline.

Used to evaluate logistics performance.

---

## Repeat Customer

A customer who has placed more than one unique order.

Used for customer retention analysis.

---

## Revenue Share

Percentage contribution of an entity (product, category, or market) to total company revenue.

Formula

```
Revenue Share % =
Entity Revenue
/
Total Revenue
×100
```
